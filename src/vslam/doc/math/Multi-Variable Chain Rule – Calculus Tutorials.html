The Multivariable Chain Rule – HMC Calculus Tutorial

Suppose that z=f(x,y)z=f(x,y), where xx and yy themselves depend on one or more variables. Multivariable Chain Rules allow us to differentiate zz with respect to any of the variables involved:

Let x=x(t)x=x(t) and y=y(t)y=y(t) be differentiable at tt and suppose that z=f(x,y)z=f(x,y) is differentiable at the point (x(t),y(t))(x(t),y(t)). Then z=f(x(t),y(t))z=f(x(t),y(t)) is differentiable at tt and

dzdt=∂z∂xdxdt+∂z∂ydydt.dzdt=∂z∂xdxdt+∂z∂ydydt.

Δz=∂z∂xΔx+∂z∂yΔy+ε1Δx+ε2ΔyΔz=∂z∂xΔx+∂z∂yΔy+ε1Δx+ε2Δy

ΔzΔt=∂z∂xΔxΔt+∂z∂yΔyΔt+ε1ΔxΔt+ε2ΔyΔt.ΔzΔt=∂z∂xΔxΔt+∂z∂yΔyΔt+ε1ΔxΔt+ε2ΔyΔt.
